Last weekend I watched a popular YouTuber spend two weeks building an inventory management system on a low-code platform. Halfway through I thought: I can build this faster, with more features, and zero platform fees.

So I did. In two days.

The result is a production-grade enterprise platform you can log into right now. Not a demo. Not a wireframe. A fully deployed application.

What's inside:

AI-powered receiving. Upload a packing slip (photo, PDF, Word, CSV, Excel) and OpenAI extracts every line item, serial number, and vendor. Plug in a handheld barcode scanner on desktop and just scan. Auto-submits, auto-advances. On mobile, the camera does the same thing. No clicking, no typing.

Encrypted vault security. Every secret is AES-256-GCM encrypted in the database. The key never lives next to the data. Steal the database file, you get nothing.

Role-based access control. Four roles with enforced segregation of duties. Not suggested. Enforced.

White-label branding. Logos, colors, favicons, theme modes. Server-side rendered, no flash. Hand it to a client and it looks like their product.

A task system filtered to your role. Pending approvals, in-progress receiving, low stock alerts, one focused view.

Full audit trail. Every action, every user, searchable and sortable.

Built-in documentation. Docusaurus ships inside the container, role-gated.

One Docker container. One command to deploy. A setup wizard handles everything.

The stack: Next.js 15, TypeScript, Prisma 7, SQLite, OpenAI, Docker. Strict OOP architecture. Every module consistent. Every endpoint audited.

Two days. Twice the features. Zero platform fees. Zero vendor lock-in.
