The Dashboard is the first page you land on when you sign in. It is designed to answer one question quickly: what does my inventory look like right now? You can use it as a daily check-in, a launch pad into other areas of the app, or a way to spot problems before they grow.

## What you can do here

- See key inventory numbers at a glance with the KPI cards.
- Review a feed of recent activity across purchasing and receiving.
- Click any card to jump straight to the relevant list or detail page.
- Spot low stock items and pending approvals without digging through menus.

## The KPI cards

At the top of the Dashboard you will see a row of KPI (key performance indicator) cards. Each card surfaces a single number so you can quickly tell whether something needs your attention.

### Total items

This card counts every distinct item in your catalog. Think of it as the size of your product list: every unique SKU your company tracks. Clicking this card takes you to the **Inventory** page so you can browse the full list.

### Low stock

The low stock card counts how many items are currently at or below their reorder threshold. When this number is zero, everything is healthy. When it is greater than zero, at least one item needs to be reordered soon. Clicking the card opens the Inventory page filtered to just the items tagged with the **LOW STOCK** badge, so you can decide what to order next.

### Pending orders

Pending orders tells you how many purchase orders are currently in flight. This includes drafts you are still writing, orders waiting on approval, and orders that have been submitted to a vendor but not yet received in full. Clicking the card takes you to the **Procurement** page so you can review the status of each one.

### Recent assets

Recent assets shows how many individual assets (serialized units) have been received in the most recent activity window. This is a quick sanity check that your receiving workflow is moving: if you expected a shipment today and the number has not moved, something may be stuck. Clicking the card takes you to a list of the latest assets so you can confirm traceability back to the purchase order that brought them in.

## Recent activity

Below the KPI cards you will find a **Recent activity** feed. This is a running log of the most important things that have happened across the platform, listed newest first. You might see entries like:

- A new purchase order was created or submitted.
- A packing slip was received and assets were added to stock.
- An item dropped below its reorder point.
- A vendor record was added or updated.

Each entry is clickable. If it references a purchase order, clicking it opens that order. If it references an item or an asset, clicking it opens the corresponding detail page. The feed is a fast way to answer "what just happened?" without hunting through multiple screens.

## How to read the Dashboard

Think of the cards and the activity feed as a loop:

1. Glance at the KPI row. If any number looks off (for example, low stock is higher than yesterday, or pending orders has climbed), that tells you where to focus.
2. Click the card that interests you to drill in.
3. When you return, skim the recent activity feed to catch up on anything that happened while you were away.

Using the Dashboard this way gives you a reliable morning routine: check the numbers, clear the alerts, then move on to your regular work.

## Tips

- The Dashboard updates automatically as you and your teammates work. You do not need to refresh the browser to see the latest numbers.
- If a KPI card shows a value you do not expect, click into it. The underlying list will always tell the full story.
- The AI assistant in the header is a great companion to the Dashboard. You can ask questions like, "Which items are low on stock?" or "Show me the most recent purchase orders." and get a written answer without leaving the page.
