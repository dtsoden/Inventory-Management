Welcome to Shane-Inventory. This guide walks you through signing in, finding your way around, and getting productive in the first few minutes.

## What you can do here

- Sign in to your account and land on the dashboard.
- Learn the layout of the app: sidebar navigation, top header bar, and main work area.
- Find the global search, the AI assistant, notifications, the theme toggle, and your profile menu.
- Get help when you need it, and sign out safely when you are done.

## Signing in

1. Open Shane-Inventory in your web browser. Your organization will provide the exact URL.
2. On the sign-in screen, enter the email address your administrator set up for you.
3. Enter your password and click **Sign In**.
4. If the credentials are valid, you will land on the **Dashboard**. If you see an error, double-check your email and password, then try again.

If you forget your password, contact your administrator to have it reset. You can change your own password later from your profile page.

## The overall layout

Once you are signed in, every page in Shane-Inventory shares the same structure. Getting comfortable with these three regions will make the rest of the app feel predictable.

### The sidebar (left)

The sidebar is the primary navigation. From here you can jump to any major area of the app:

- **Dashboard** gives you an at-a-glance view of inventory health, open orders, and recent activity.
- **Inventory** lists every item your company stocks, with low stock warnings.
- **Procurement** is where you create and manage purchase orders.
- **Receiving** is where you check in packing slips and scan asset tags.
- **Vendors** lets you browse and maintain your supplier records.

The sidebar can usually be collapsed to icons only if you want more room for the main content.

### The header bar (top)

Across the top of every page you will find a set of tools that stay with you no matter where you are:

- **Global search.** Type a keyword to quickly find items, vendors, or purchase orders without clicking through menus.
- **AI assistant.** A chat button opens the assistant, which can answer questions about your inventory in plain English. See the AI Assistant guide for examples.
- **Notifications.** A bell icon shows alerts such as low stock warnings, approvals waiting on you, or orders that have been received.
- **Theme toggle.** Switch between light and dark mode whenever you like; your choice is remembered next time you sign in.
- **Profile menu.** Your avatar in the top right opens a small menu with a link to your profile page, help, and sign out.

### The main work area (center)

Everything else is the page you are working on. Lists, forms, item details, and dashboards all render here. Most pages follow the same conventions: filters and search at the top, a list or grid in the middle, and action buttons (such as **New**, **Edit**, or **Save**) in clearly labeled spots.

## Finding help

Help is always close by.

- Click the **AI assistant** in the header and ask a question, for example, "How do I create a purchase order?" The assistant can walk you through workflows and answer questions about your current data.
- The **Help** link in the profile menu opens the user documentation you are reading right now.
- If something looks wrong or you cannot complete a task, contact your administrator. They can check permissions, reset your password, or adjust settings on your behalf.

## Signing out

When you are finished working, it is a good habit to sign out, especially on a shared computer.

1. Click your avatar in the top right corner of the header.
2. Choose **Sign Out** from the menu.
3. You will be returned to the sign-in screen. Close your browser tab or window to finish.

## Next steps

Now that you are signed in and oriented, the rest of the user guide covers each area in depth. A good path for new users is:

1. Read the **Dashboard** guide to understand the KPI cards and recent activity.
2. Browse the **Inventory** guide to learn how items, stock, and assets relate.
3. Walk through the **Procurement** and **Receiving** guides together, since they describe the full purchase lifecycle.
4. Skim the **Vendors**, **AI Assistant**, and **Your Profile** guides for the supporting features you will use every day.
