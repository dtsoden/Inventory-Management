Vendors are the companies you buy from. The Vendors page is your directory of suppliers, their contact details, the items you buy from each one, and the history of every order you have placed with them.

## What you can do here

- Browse every vendor your company does business with.
- Open a vendor card to see full contact details and history.
- Add a new vendor or edit an existing one.
- See the items you typically source from each vendor.
- Review the orders you have placed with each vendor.

## Browsing vendors

Open **Vendors** from the sidebar. You will see a card-based list of every vendor on file, each card showing the vendor's name, logo (if uploaded), primary contact, and a short summary. Use the search box at the top of the page to find a specific vendor by name, and use filters if you want to narrow the list by category or status.

Click a card to open the vendor's full detail page.

## The vendor card

Each vendor card, whether in the list view or the detail view, shows the information you need most:

- The vendor's **name** and a short description.
- The primary **contact person**, **email**, and **phone number**.
- The **address** used on shipping and billing.
- A **logo**, if the vendor has provided one.
- Any **notes** your team has added about the relationship.

On the detail page, these fields are grouped into sections so the page stays readable even when there is a lot of information.

## Adding a new vendor

1. Open **Vendors** and click **New Vendor**.
2. Fill in the vendor name. This is the only required field to get started, but the more you fill in now, the less you will have to chase later.
3. Add the primary contact name, email, and phone number.
4. Add the billing and shipping addresses.
5. Optionally upload a logo and write a description or notes.
6. Click **Save**. The vendor appears in the list immediately and is available on new purchase orders.

## Editing a vendor

Vendor information changes over time: people move, companies rebrand, addresses shift. To keep your records current:

1. Open the vendor card.
2. Click **Edit**.
3. Update any field that has changed.
4. Click **Save** to apply your changes.

Edits take effect immediately. Existing purchase orders are not altered retroactively; they keep the details that were in place when the order was created, so your history stays accurate.

## The vendor's items

Each vendor detail page includes an **Items** section that lists the products you regularly buy from this supplier. This is a quick way to answer, "What do we source from this vendor?"

For each item you can see:

- The item name, manufacturer, and model.
- The current quantity you have on hand.
- The last price you paid this vendor for it.
- A link into the item's full detail page on the Inventory side.

This section is populated automatically from your purchase history. As you place more orders, the list grows to reflect what you actually buy from each supplier.

## The vendor's orders

The **Orders** section on the vendor detail page lists every purchase order you have ever placed with this vendor, newest first. For each order you can see:

- The PO number.
- The date.
- The status (Draft, Pending Approval, Approved, Submitted, Received, Cancelled).
- The total amount.

Click any row to open the full purchase order. This is the fastest way to answer questions like "When did we last buy from this vendor?" or "What did we pay on the last order?" without leaving the vendor page.

## Typical workflows

### "I need to onboard a new supplier."

1. Open **Vendors** and click **New Vendor**.
2. Enter their name, contacts, and address.
3. Save. You can now create purchase orders against this vendor.

### "Our main contact at a vendor just changed."

1. Open the vendor card.
2. Click **Edit**.
3. Update the contact name, email, and phone.
4. Save. The next PO you create will carry the updated contact details.

### "Which vendor has the best price on this item?"

1. Open the **Inventory** page and find the item.
2. Use the **Purchase History** tab to compare prices across vendors, or.
3. Open each vendor from the Vendors page and check the item's price in their **Items** section.

## Tips

- Keep vendor notes short and factual (for example, "Ships from East Coast, 3-day lead time"). They are visible to everyone on your team and help future users make good decisions.
- Uploading a vendor logo is optional, but it makes the list scan much faster.
- If you stop doing business with a vendor, leave the record in place rather than deleting it. The history of past purchase orders is valuable for audits and reporting.
